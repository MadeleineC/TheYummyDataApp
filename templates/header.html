    Yummy Data Map FAQ
    What is the YummyData?

    YummyData is the easiest way to identify the best area to launch your new restaurant to reach the most success.


    How Does YummyData work?

    Simply give us 3 things: your city, your state, and your cuisine type. We'll give you a map that shows current competition, population, and income level per each zipcode in your targetted area!


    What is visible through the YummyMap?

    The YummyMap will provide the client with the exact points of competitors in their area, the average income of the area, along with the population density to allow for a precursory view of potential areas for launching a new restaurant or expansion!


    Why is Waco, Tx autoloading?

    We wanted to do our part to bring awareness to the fact that Waco, Tx, home of the Dr Pepper Museum, actuall has a good number of sushi restaurants.


    Why is nothing loading?

    Searches for larger cities that are not currently in the database may result in timeouts when we're gathering the data. This is a limitation from the free hosting site we used, not of our program.


    Where are you getting your numbers from?

    Income & population levels are sourced from a python module which sourced their data on the 2010 census.


    Denny's doesnt serve tacos!

    Contrary to popular belief, Denny's is actually quite accomodating.
    Our restaurant results are sourced from Google Places...so if they say Denny's has tacos, well, Google knows all.


    Every zipcode in my search is impoverished!

    Income data was normalized so that the income levels you're seeing are compared to the national average.
